Conclusion and Suggestions

1. Conclusion

The 20-week industrial training at Dynamic Traffic System Sdn Bhd (DynaTech) has been a transformative experience that successfully bridged the gap between academic theory at Politeknik Sultan Idris Shah and professional practice. Working within the AI department allowed me to contribute to high-impact projects, specifically the Radar Digital Twin and the modernization of the mobile application ecosystem.

Through the development of 3D junction models in Unity and the implementation of Jetpack Compose in Android development, I have mastered the "Design-to-Code" workflow. Furthermore, my involvement in YOLO-based vehicle classification and backend integration using PostgreSQL and PHP APIs has provided me with a full-stack perspective on how AI and IoT solutions solve real-world traffic management challenges. This internship has not only sharpened my technical proficiency in Kotlin, C#, and Python but also instilled a disciplined approach to version control and system documentation.

2. Suggestions for the Company

  • Adoption of Standardized Version Control (Git): Suggesting the official implementation of Git/GitHub for all current and future software projects. Transitioning away from saving code locally on individual machines prevents accidental data loss, tracks changes clearly, and ensures the company always has a secure, master backup of its source code.
  • Standardized Project Onboarding Documentation: Requiring a basic "Readme" text file for every active project repository. This document would outline what the project does and how to run it locally, allowing future developers or interns to take over the project seamlessly without requiring extensive retraining.

3. Suggestions for the Institution (PSIS)

  • Modern Framework Exposure: I recommend incorporating modern declarative UI frameworks like Jetpack Compose or Flutter into the mobile development curriculum, as these are now industry standards and significantly improve development efficiency over traditional XML layouts.
  • Industry-Standard VCS Training: While basic Git is taught, a deeper focus on professional branching strategies (like GitFlow) and collaborative code reviews would better prepare students for the fast-paced environment of a professional software engineering team.

Final Closing

In conclusion, I am deeply grateful to my supervisor and the team at DynaTech for their mentorship. The skills acquired during this tenure have solidified my foundation as a Digital Technology professional, and I look forward to applying this expertise to future innovations in the tech industry.
